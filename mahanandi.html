Mahanandi Temple


                 

Description

The Mahanandi Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, is located in Kurnool district of Andhra Pradesh. It is known for its unique architecture and sacred significance, attracting devotees from all over the region.

Built:

Dates back to the 5th century during the reign of the Western Ganga dynasty.

Deity:

The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva in the form of a Linga, known as Mahanandi.

Video:

Architectural Features

Style:

The temple showcases Dravidian architecture with intricate carvings and sculptures, reflecting the artistic brilliance of the era.

Main Shrine:

The shrine houses the Mahanandi Linga, which is believed to have healing powers.

Intricate Carvings:

Visitors can admire beautiful carvings of deities and mythological scenes adorning the temple walls and pillars.

Wear modest clothing that covers shoulders and knees to show respect in the temple.
